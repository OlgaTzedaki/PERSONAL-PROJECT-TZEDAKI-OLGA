I personally love reading the Insider, The New York Times, Time and The Guardian. I think that going through the articles serves as an excellent way of really seeing how English is used in a realistic way. Even though I have already taken the CPE exam, I always want to keep my language skills sharp. The aforementioned websites include articles written by professionals and I consider them a great lexical resource, aside from reliable beacons in today's world of misinformation. If you're looking for more "lightweight" themes, be sure to check out Popsugar and Buzzfeed. The latter is where I attribute my being able to understand the internet and Gen Z's slang! The sub-section Buzzfeed News contains full - length articles while the posts mainly include Twitter and Reddit posts. Also, as an amateur speaker of french, I've been trying to read french Vogue articles more often. What I found is that they truly aid me in getting the meaning of words more quickly, as I've grown accustomed to seeing some of them used in specific contexts and accompanying others. After all, french are a rapidly-spoken language, which makes it challenging for non-natives to achieve full language immersion. Lastly, if you're interested in the more technical aspect of things, Makeuseof, Macworld and pcmag are some of my favorite websites that keep me informed about all things tech.

I hope this helps :)
